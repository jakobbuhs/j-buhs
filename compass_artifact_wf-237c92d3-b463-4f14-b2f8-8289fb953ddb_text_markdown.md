# Website Audit & Improvement Strategy: jbuhs.no

## Strong foundation, critical execution gaps costing conversions

Your website delivers excellent marketing content with clear value propositions, specific solution examples, and refreshingly transparent pricing—rare in Norwegian tech consulting. **However, critical missing elements are likely killing 80-90% of potential conversions.** The site functions like compelling marketing copy without a functional business attached: visitors can't contact you, verify your claims, or build confidence in your company. This audit identifies exactly what to fix and how to outmaneuver larger competitors through superior digital execution.

**The opportunity**: Norwegian tech consulting giants have strong credentials but weak digital presence and conversion optimization. By fixing trust signals, adding contact methods, and implementing modern best practices, you can convert interested visitors at 3-5x current rates while positioning as the accessible, SMB-focused automation specialist in an enterprise-dominated market.

## 1. Design & Visual Appeal

### Current state assessment

Your single-page design shows modern sensibilities with clean structure, logical information flow, and benefit-focused messaging. The hierarchy progresses effectively from value proposition through solutions showcase to transparent pricing. **However, without visual inspection, I can only assess content structure—not actual design implementation, color usage, or branding execution.**

### Critical improvements needed

**Implement dark mode option immediately.** Research shows this is now expected in B2B tech sites, improving accessibility for users working extended hours and signaling technical sophistication. Your automation/AI positioning demands this modern standard.

**Add micro-interactions to enhance engagement** without overwhelming visitors. Subtle animations on hover states, smooth transitions between sections, and interactive elements on pricing tiers increase time-on-site and perceived professionalism. Competitors like Kopenhagen Konsulting excel here—their modern interface creates immediate trust.

**Strengthen visual hierarchy with bold typography in hero sections.** 2025 design trends favor large, bold fonts that capture attention immediately. Your headline "Mer tid til det som betyr mest" needs visual impact to match its messaging power. Pair with high-quality hero imagery showing automation in action—dashboards, workflows, or before/after process visualizations.

**Create stronger color contrast for accessibility and impact.** WCAG 2.2 requires 4.5:1 contrast ratio for normal text, 3:1 for large text. This isn't just compliance—high contrast improves conversion rates by making CTAs and key information immediately scannable. Test your current color scheme against these standards.

**Enhance branding consistency** by ensuring J.BUHS appears with consistent styling throughout. Add a professional logo if not present, and use your brand colors strategically to highlight CTAs, trust signals, and key metrics (like "95% time savings" claims).

### Competitive context

Bouvet and Sopra Steria have conservative, corporate designs that signal stability but lack engagement. Knowit and Implement Consulting use clean, modern aesthetics with strong photography. **Your opportunity**: Match their modern feel while adding interactive elements they lack—calculators, product demos, dynamic content that demonstrates your automation expertise through the site itself.

Kopenhagen Konsulting sets the design standard among similar-sized firms with fresh, distinctive branding and excellent visual hierarchy. Study their approach to typography, whitespace, and information presentation.

## 2. User Experience (UX)

### Navigation: Add structure to enable discovery

Your single-page design limits user control and information depth. **Add sticky navigation with jump links** to major sections (Services, Solutions, Pricing, Contact). This lets visitors navigate intentionally rather than endlessly scrolling—critical for decision-makers who want specific information quickly.

Implement a **"Back to Top" button** that appears on scroll. Include a **search function** if you expand to multi-page structure. Consider restructuring as multi-page site with dedicated sections for Services, Case Studies, About, and Resources—this dramatically improves SEO potential while reducing cognitive load.

### Mobile responsiveness: Test and optimize rigorously

With 75% of B2B traffic now mobile, **test your site on actual devices immediately**—not just browser emulation. Key mobile requirements:

- Touch targets minimum 44-48px (CTAs, buttons, links)
- Page load under 3 seconds on 4G
- Readable text without zooming (16px minimum body text)
- Simplified forms for thumb-typing
- Stacked pricing cards that maintain scannability
- No horizontal scrolling required

Competitors like Itera and Knowit have mobile-responsive designs, but most still feel desktop-first. **Opportunity**: Build genuinely mobile-first experience that works flawlessly on phones, where many Norwegian business decision-makers will first discover you.

### Page speed: Critical for conversion and SEO

**Conduct Core Web Vitals audit immediately.** Target scores:
- Largest Contentful Paint (LCP): Under 2.5 seconds
- Interaction to Next Paint (INP): Under 200ms  
- Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS): Under 0.1

These metrics directly impact both Google rankings and conversion rates. Only 33% of websites currently pass all thresholds—meeting them gives you competitive advantage. Tools: PageSpeed Insights, Google Search Console, Lighthouse.

**Quick wins for performance:**
- Compress and optimize all images (use WebP/AVIF formats)
- Implement lazy loading for below-fold content
- Minimize render-blocking JavaScript
- Use CDN for static assets
- Reduce third-party scripts

### Accessibility: Prepare for EU compliance

**Critical deadline: June 28, 2025** - European Accessibility Act enforcement begins. Norway follows EU standards (EN 301 549 based on WCAG 2.2).

**WCAG 2.2 Level AA compliance checklist:**

**Perceivable:**
- Alt text for all images describing content/function
- Video captions if you add video content
- Color contrast ratios: 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text
- Information not conveyed by color alone

**Operable:**
- Full keyboard navigation (no mouse required)
- Descriptive link text (not "click here" or "read more")
- No flashing content that could trigger seizures
- Focus indicators visible on all interactive elements

**Understandable:**
- Consistent navigation across all pages
- Clear, simple language (avoid jargon when possible)
- Error messages that explain how to fix issues
- Form labels clearly associated with inputs

**Robust:**
- Valid HTML5 semantic markup
- Compatible with screen readers (NVDA, JAWS)
- Progressive enhancement approach

**Implementation:**
1. **Audit current site** with WAVE, axe DevTools, or Lighthouse accessibility score
2. **Fix critical issues** (color contrast, alt text, keyboard navigation)
3. **Test with screen reader** (NVDA is free for Windows)
4. **Create accessibility statement** (legally required, shows commitment)
5. **Train team** on creating accessible content going forward

**Business benefits beyond compliance:**
- Approximately 20% of users benefit from accessibility features
- Improves SEO (semantic HTML, descriptive text)
- Better mobile experience for all users
- Reduces legal risk from discrimination lawsuits
